Project Idea.

Overview

The project will be a health diagnoses application that will give health diagnoses and advice based on the user’s health data and described symptoms.  It will use an android application to provide a broad range of features that are usually separated into different applications. These services will involve health advice, health tracking, and discussion forums, which will provide benefits to the user and consulted medical professionals. Health advice will offer preventative and reactive medical advice which enables the user to improve health whilst preventing and treating illnesses.

To provide the high-quality advice, artificial intelligence and cloud computing services will be used to store and analyse medical data which advises based on the latest medical standards. Additionally, forums will be used to discuss health, fitness, and general topics which will provide a social and engaging atmosphere. The application is intended as a tool to be used in conjunction with the appropriate healthcare professionals should be consulted about the information provided by the application.

Scopes and limits:

The scope is rather huge with this application with its consistent monitoring and diagnosis of user's medical issues. This application will ever be expanding with content due to various medical diseases and issues, evermore so in today's climate with the ongoing coronavirus pandemic this application would greatly reduce the need for “corona scans'' where users will be able to do it at home reducing the risk of transmission and so on. This application will have an ever-expanding scope due to the never-ending medical material in today's world. The application will first identify the symptoms and diagnose the user with the resulting diagnosis and then go on to display an array of treatments that the user can purchase or perform and in server cases see a medical practitioner. Taking into consideration the time restraints to fully develop this app we shall be limiting ourselves to one medical problem where the user inputs their symptoms and the AI determines the medical diagnosis for example “the common cold” as this is a common disease which affects millions around the world. The application has an infinite amount of scope as this application would ever be expanding with more information, more diagnosis, and more features such as “doctor chats” and much more.

Risks

With a project of this scale, there is no doubting the risks that may be involved. From how the products received by the audience to expectations of certain applications/software not functioning in the way its expected or hoped to. Not having too much experience in developing apps, the project faces risks in terms of development of the needed software to hold and collect all the health information of all its users in the one cloud.

Health systems already exist to store all client’s health records and information; therefore, it becomes more about integration of our developed app with said existing systems. We could potentially face security or privacy risks with this in mind as our apps system would rely on access to an abundance of medical data and records, possibly transferred through cloud computing. Due to the app using, storing, and maintaining highly confidential information the biggest risk is data leakage.

Manufacturing our app specific hardware is quite important for the app to perform at full force in union with our hardware, for example, custom design smartwatches, fall-support-belt, and the concept smart pill. Whilst the concept of custom designed technology to support our application sounds beneficial, there is great risk in developing them as quickly as the consumer may need or want. Due to a lack of knowledge or experience in hardware development, without hiring external forces that have the knowledge, we could encounter a lot of faulty technology design which could prove detrimental. With that in mind, it's no secret there is a massive amount of funding that is required for such technology, as well as the costing for the rest of the project, such as paying developers or funding the software development. Taking on hardware manufacturing early is a great risk to the group financially and timewise on its own alongside everything else that needs developing to produce an app and system that works to a user standard.

Group processes and communication

Communication throughout the group is pivotal in mission success and plays a huge part in reaching the project goals we set for ourselves along the way. Our team stays quite connected through MS Teams as well as emailing so holding communication in high regards throughout the process is a no-brainer. Already being aware of the consequences that occur from lack of staying in touch, face-to-face meetings are detrimental throughout our weeks to keep eir sole tasks. A Minimum of 1 face-to-face meeting over MS-team’s is required per week to keep the team on track and on the same page noting any progression or mishaps they may have with certain areas inside the project. If a member of the group is not reachable at the time they are needed, tagging them in all messages or updates and emailing said person is a good idea to keep them updated once they do become available or return, whatever the case may be. This then provides the opportunity for a falling behind team-mate who may be lacking on communication to catch-up and speed themselves up to scratch once they have seen what they have missed. Discussing amongst the group and determining times and days suited to everyone to catch up face-to-face for meetings during each of our weeks will keep everyone connected enough to form a plan and method of commitment individually, and with everyone’s efforts combined it will help bring our project to life. With organised, set up meetings booked in through MS teams, each member of the team will know when they need to be available to meet by viewing their MS Teams calendar and acknowledging what days and time’s meetings are each week. All of which can be rescheduled or revaluated and should have gained approval from all members of the group before setting up new scheduled meetings.

Group Reflection: Team

Group’s perspective

We were a group of four, all with busy lives and commitments outside of this unit Introduction to Information Technology. When working on a project as part of a team in a workplace, meeting can be scheduled a lot easier amongst colleagues. When studying on campus again it is easy to meet up after or before class. Online learning is a completely different settling, with a far narrower window of opportunities. Not everyone attends online lectures at the same time as online offers flexibility.

What went well

The group’s communication and allocating of tasks was a smooth process. We summarised and put all the required tasks in a document for people to pick and record which parts of the assignment they would like to complete. Using teams was especially useful for recording this as multiple people could alter the same form detailing contributions. This was functionally important due to us mainly using messaging as a means of communication. If people did not record which tasks they were doing then it could result in group members completing the same tasks.

In addition to choosing individual contributions, the team also chose an appropriate workload of contribution from the tasks available in the document. This allowed for people to choose their tasks according to their preferences and skill sets. For example, Anish had previously worked on website for assignment 2, it was efficient for them to also complete the website for assignment 3.

Detailing the sections as described in the assignment the tasks were distributed below:

James: Overview (topic, motivation, landscape) + Detailed description (aims, plans and progress, roles) + Group’s (group) perspective

Aaron: Skills & jobs + Detailed description (timeframe)

Anish: Tools + presentation + Detailed description (limits, tools and tech, testing, project idea artefact) + Assignment 5 script

Jacob: Team profile + detailed description (Group processes and communication, Risks) + Assignment 5 script and presentation

Everyone put in and worked hard to get the job done. As the tasks were not worth equal marks, some members ended up doing more than others. This is how group project works even in the workforce. This is not how it should work but is how most teamwork projects end up working if they require constant input by all members.

What could be improved

In the early weeks of assignment 3, we all contributed ideas and suggestions for the assignment improvements and changes to the project idea described in our assignment 2. During this time is would have been wise to also discuss the limitations on creating artefacts for assignment 3. Many of our members lacked the skills required to develop interactive applications which could have resulted in us changing our project idea or brainstorming more about which artefacts would be feasible.

At least one thing that was surprising

One thing our group found surprising was how much we could accomplish despite our different availability for Teams meetings. Although we set early concrete meeting times, hiccups occurred in member schedules, which lead to cancellations. We were initially worried about our ability to collaborate due to different availability of times for Teams meetings, but eventually realised the effectiveness of how much we could accomplish in a short period of time by effectively distributing tasks. Although assignment 3 was quite large and we also had to prepare the assignment 5 presentation, we all managed to contribute to the assignment.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups

When you only have four weeks to complete an assignment a week is a long time and things to not always go to plan. Our group learned the importance of making progress earlier. We realised that along the way we have lost group members and that if additional group members left during assignment 3 then the already large task, for our group of 4, would become less manageable. We were fortunate that our team continued to make contributions throughout the study period and stayed until the end.
